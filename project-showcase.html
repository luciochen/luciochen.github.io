Tool library showcase

Overview

The Tool library showcase is a platform for the Vancouver tool library to educate members tool literacy with its featured handcraft projects.

In this project, we implemented research tools to develop our understanding of the clients. Finally, we came up with a design solution to enhance the workshop engagement and keep members interested in lending tools.


Role

  • User research
  • UX/UI design
  • Graphic design

Context

Course project: Interaction Design Methods (Spring 2020)

Group with Caleb, T., Dawood, S., and Fatima, I.

Tools: Figma, Illustrator, Photoshop

Our client & Objective

The Vancouver tool library strives to make Vancouver more sustainable by lending out tools. It also holds handcrafting workshops to teach members how to DIY an artifact with these tools.

Our group aimed to find out the pain points in the interaction within this organization, and tried to deliver a better in-store/project experience for the members.

Vancouver Tool Library, images from: https://www.instagram.com/p/B_lb68CB3dP/

Research

As part of our group's research, I conveyed in-person interviews with 4 volunteers and few members to understand their needs and pain points in this organization.

After the research, our group found a large amount of amateur members are experienced on neither using tools nor doing projects, which raised our concern about members' safety and tool maintenance. Therefore, we chose to focus on creating a smooth tool renting experience that ensures the safety of new/amateur users.

User journey map highlight

Participatory Workshop

When designing the participatory workshop, I considered how to make participants engage in the activities well and co-create with us. Therefore, I designed the warm-up questions and activities flow to make participants smoothly transit to the state of co-creating.

During the workshop, participants suggested a common need to educate amateur members with more project practice. Also, we learned that the tool safety from the initial focus should be avoided because it will become a liability.

Conclusion

Working closely with the Vancouver Tool Library, it helps me to understand how to research and design to tackle a real problem. It allows me to practice many research methods to get a clear understanding of the goals and near problems from both stakeholders and users. In the process, I always value the data gathered from the research. Finally, we delivered a feasible solution to help with this organization.

Reflection

The product on the Vancouver tool library's main page is not very explicit, it causes a problem when it is hard to get people exposed to our product. Therefore, It would be better if there is an in-store promotion to get more people to use this project showcase.

Lastly, the situation of COVID-19 gave us many constraints. If things went offline, I will conduct a participatory workshop with more engaging activities. We missed the opportunity for the product user testing, which could be valuable if we test out our to know if the members will be engaged in handcraft projects more with our product.
