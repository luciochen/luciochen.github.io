Overview

The Tool library showcase is a platform for the Vancouver tool library to educate members about tool literacy with its featured handcraft projects.

In this project, we implemented research tools to develop our understanding of the clients. Finally, we came up with a design solution to enhance the workshop engagement and keep members interested in lending tools.

The Client

The Vancouver tool library strives to make Vancouver more sustainable by lending out tools. It also holds handcrafting workshops to teach members how to DIY an artifact with these tools.

Vancouver Tool Library, images from: https://www.instagram.com/p/B_lb68CB3dP/

User Research

During the research, I conducted in-person interviews with 4 volunteers and a few store members. As a result, the research indicates the problem that many members are not experienced in doing DIY projects with tools.

User journey map highlight

Design Focus

After the research, we directed our design focus to enhance new and amateur member’s tool literacy through workshop engagement and projects.

Based on members' active engagement with the Tool library website, we finally chose the solution of a DIY project showcase, which provides the members a platform to practice using tools when replicating the projects following given instructions.

Conclusion

Working closely with Vancouver Tool Library, I practiced user research in this real business and understand the problem from the users and stakeholders' perspective. As a result, the manager of the store likes the design and was convienced that the design can be used as a good reference to develop a website section.

Reflection

However, we found that the showcase section on the website's home page is not very explicit and hard to get noticed. Therefore, there is an opportunity to increase the exposure of our design solution by promoting our design and getting the users exposed to this showcase section during their interaction with the Tool Library.
